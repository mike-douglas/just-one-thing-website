# Just One Thing

## Voice & Vocabulary Guide

`v1.1 — Internal Reference`

-----

## 01 — Voice Principles

Just One Thing is a silent tool. It executes. It doesn’t coach, cheer, or editorialize. The product’s confidence comes from what it does, not what it says about itself.

**The three rules**

1. Speak only when the system needs to. Labels, states, actions. Nothing motivational, nothing reactive.
1. Literal over charged. Describe what is happening, not what it means.
1. No second-person coaching. The app is not a voice in the user’s head.

-----

## 02 — Core Vocabulary

Canonical terms for the app’s core concepts. Use them consistently across UI, App Store copy, onboarding, and marketing. Do not invent synonyms.

|Term            |Definition & Usage                                                                                                                                                                            |
|----------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Start**       |The primary action verb. The user starts a session. Used on the CTA button and in onboarding. Literal, zero baggage. Do not substitute "lock in", "begin", "go", or "commit".|
|**Task**        |The thing the user commits to. Not “goal”, “focus”, “intention”, or “mission”. One word, no weight.                                                                                           |
|**Blocked**     |State of apps during a session. “Apps blocked.” Not “restricted”, “paused”, “locked out”, or “disabled”.                                                                                      |
|**Session**     |The period between starting and marking done. Avoid “focus session”, “work block”, or “grind”.                                                                                              |
|**Mark as done**|The completion action. Not “I’m done”, “finish”, “complete task”, or “end session”.                                                                                                           |
|**Streak**      |Consecutive days with at least one completed session. Shown on the completion screen. No emoji or exclamation in the label itself.                                                            |
|**Unlock**      |What happens to apps after marking done. “Unlocks all apps.” Not “freedom”, “release”, or “apps restored”.                                                                                    |

## 05 — What the App Never Says

Not a fitness coach. Not a productivity guru. Not a hype machine. The following are permanently off-limits regardless of context, screen, or marketing placement.

- ✗ You’ve got this!
- ✗ Stay focused!
- ✗ Keep grinding.
- ✗ Beast mode.
- ✗ No excuses.
- ✗ Crush it.
- ✗ You’re killing it!
- ✗ Amazing work!
- ✗ We believe in you.
- ✗ Unlock your potential.
- ✗ Be your best self.
- ✗ This is your time.
- ✗ The hustle is real.
- ✗ Winners don’t quit.

## 06 — Tone by Context

The voice stays consistent but the register shifts slightly depending on where copy appears.

**In-App UI**
Terse. Functional. No personality. Every word has a job. If it doesn’t label, instruct, or report — cut it.

**App Store Listing**
Slightly more voice, still minimal. Lead with what the app does, not how it makes you feel. Facts over promises. Avoid superlatives. One sharp headline, then evidence.

> *“One task. Apps blocked until it’s done. No AI cameras. No judgment. Just a commitment device that works.”*

**Push Notifications**
If used at all, keep to pure utility. Session reminders, streak at risk. Never motivational. Maximum 6 words.

> ✓ *“You have no task set today.”*
> ✗ *“Don’t break your streak — lock in now! 🔥”*

**Onboarding**
Explain the mechanic, not the philosophy. Users don’t need to be sold on focus — they downloaded the app. Get them to their first session as fast as possible.
